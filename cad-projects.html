    Airplane CAD Model

    WWII-style aircraft modeled for aerodynamic design, SolidWorks surface features, and assembly planning.

    Project Details

    Constructed fuselage and wings using Siemens NX surface modeling techniques with a focus on aerodynamic performance. Digitized and positioned cross-sectional curves based on historical aircraft data. Created watertight surfaces using Through Curve, Bridge Surface, and Edge Blend tools. Performed Gaussian and highlight line curvature analysis to ensure flow continuity. Final model exhibited zero inflection points and verified aerodynamic surface quality.

    Robotic Arm

    Articulated robotic arm using parametric laws and curve-based constraints. Features multi-axis joint simulations.

    Project Details

    This project analyzed a 3D serpentine curve to determine curvature, trajectory length, and maximum acceleration, simulating robotic arm motion. Used Siemens NX for surface modeling and MATLAB for curvature simulation. Computed max normal acceleration of 0.44 m/s² at (−465.93, 441.42, 113.74). Trajectory length increased from 7856 mm to 47128 mm by modifying curve frequency and amplitude.

    Drone Assembly

    Modular drone model with detailed part files including propeller, drive gear, shafts, and full mechanical layout.

    Project Details

    Developed a full drone assembly from individual parts including arm, drive gear, propeller, and housing. Each part was dimensioned and modeled for proper fit and kinematic alignment. Assembly involved coordinating concentric, coincident, and distance constraints to ensure structural stability and spatial logic. This modular system highlights design for manufacturing and 3D-printability.
